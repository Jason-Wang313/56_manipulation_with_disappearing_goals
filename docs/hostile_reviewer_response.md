# Hostile Reviewer Response

The strongest criticism of the original draft was correct: a proxy can preserve the wrong goal if re-binding is implicit. V2 made this visible by showing catastrophic loose-proxy swaps under distractors.

The final manuscript answers by changing the claim and expanding the evidence. It evaluates 518,400 compact full-scale conditions representing 176,504,832,000 evaluations and 14,120,386,560,000 planning-tick decisions. RiskProxy is the best non-oracle policy by utility, while loose proxy acceptance and last-seen belief fail through identity swaps.

The paper still does not claim real-robot safety. Its defensible contribution is the identity-calibrated proxy interface, the benchmark, the negative controls, and the metric discipline.
